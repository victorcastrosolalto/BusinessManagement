Networks & The Internet

Foundations of Digital Society

Core Understanding

Networks and the internet are the fundamental infrastructure of digital society, profoundly impacting our daily lives. By the end of this unit, you should understand their essential nature, evolution, and dual impact on modern life.

Connectivity & Distributed Systems

Networks connect computers, people, and communities, enabling data and information to be created, accessed, and shared in a distributed manner. Information isn't stored in one central location but is spread across various connected devices, allowing for widespread access and collaboration.

Without a school Wi-Fi network, accessing lesson resources, uploading homework, or searching for assignment information would be impossible. This highlights our dependency on these connections.

Evolution of Digital Society

Networks and the internet are defining features of digital society that have evolved over time. They are not static technologies but have continuously developed, shaping how we interact and live.

We'll explore network technologies, their configuration, the internet, and the World Wide Web. We'll also examine whether Sir Tim Berners-Lee's vision for the World Wide Web is being met in today's digital landscape.

From ARPANET to 5G, the evolution of networking technologies has transformed society at an unprecedented pace.

Opportunities & Dilemmas

Networks and the internet present significant opportunities and dilemmas for life in digital society. While they offer immense benefits, they also introduce challenges and risks that need to be considered.

Our daily lives are profoundly affected by networks from the moment we wake up until we charge our phones at night. This ubiquitous presence makes our lives dependent on various networks for education, communication, work, and entertainment.

Issues like privacy concerns, digital divide, and cybersecurity threats represent the dilemmas we face in our networked society.

The Highway System Analogy

Think of networks and the internet as a vast, intricate highway system. The "Core Understanding" is like grasping why this highway system exists: it connects everyone and everything, has been built and expanded over many years, and while it brings incredible opportunities for travel and commerce, it also comes with traffic jams and safety concerns that need to be managed.
