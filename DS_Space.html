Space in Digital Society

Exploring the transformation of physical, virtual, and hybrid spaces through digital technologies

IB Digital Society Section 2.5 Pages 39-40

Defining Space

Humans organize, construct, and represent space based on:

  • Physical features (locations, regions)
  • Geographic elements (borders, zones)
  • Cultural and social characteristics

Virtual Spaces

  • Increasingly created and utilized in digital society
  • Diverse and distinct from traditional spaces
  • Becoming essential for daily operations

Types of Spaces

Scales & Dimensions

  • Local
  • Regional
  • National
  • Global
  • Virtual (increasingly significant)

Space Purpose

Spaces serve distinct functions for individuals and communities:

  • Essential for all human operations
  • Constructed for specific daily functions
  • Virtual spaces growing in importance

Interactions in Space

In both real and virtual spaces:

  • People, information, and objects interact
  • Complex flows of movement and access
  • Interaction through content access and transitions

Digital Transformation

  • Digital technologies alter space experience
  • Impact on objects in virtual/real spaces
  • Changing relationship with physical locations

Critical Questions

Physical vs. Virtual Space

  • How do online and physical spaces differ? How are they similar?
  • How does physical space influence virtual space, and vice versa?

Borders & Divisions

  • Do physical/political borders retain meaning in digital society?
  • How are borders and laws changing in virtual spaces?
  • Are digital divides comparable to physical borders?

Information Flow

  • How do information and digital objects circulate?
  • How does this differ from circulation in physical spaces?

ATL Activities

Thinking Activity: Personal Reflection

  • What virtual spaces do you inhabit?
  • How have digital technologies enhanced your use of physical spaces?
  • How have virtual spaces impacted your education, relationships, and work?
  • What are the advantages and dangers of your virtual spaces?

Research Activity: Virtual Space Impacts

Investigate positive and negative impacts of:

  • Virtual & Augmented Reality
  • Online political boundaries
  • Encryption technologies
  • Data legislation & boundaries
  • Cryptocurrencies
  • Political use of cyberspace
