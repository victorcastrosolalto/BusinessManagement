Quality Control vs. Quality Assurance

The Foundational Shift in Quality Management
Quality Control (QC)
Reactive approach: Inspecting quality after production
Concept
Quality is "controlled" by one person inspecting finished products after production. Checks are performed to ensure products meet desired quality levels.
Costs
Accepts a percentage of rejects (e.g., 2% failure rate). This approach leads to wasteful production and rework costs.
Processes
Rarely halts production due to cost concerns. Focuses only on the immediate task with no consideration for suppliers or after-sales service.
People
Quality is one person's responsibility. Associated with autocratic leadership and top-down communication in a "role culture".
Quality Assurance (QA)
Proactive approach: Building quality into every process
Concept
Quality is "assured" throughout the organization. A system of setting and monitoring quality standards across the entire production process.
Costs
Zero rejects expected. Aligns with lean production principles to eliminate waste and reduce costs long-term.
Processes
Halts production to fix errors when necessary. Considers suppliers and after-sales service with a holistic view of the production chain.
People
Quality is the team's responsibility (through "quality circles"). Fosters a "total quality culture" with democratic leadership and 360° communication.
The House Building Analogy
Quality Control Approach

Inspecting the finished house for flaws:

  • Checking if paint is even
  • Verifying doors close properly
  • Testing plumbing after installation

Problems are fixed after construction is complete.

Quality Assurance Approach

Inspecting at every stage of construction:

  • Checking foundation as it's poured
  • Verifying lumber before framing
  • Ensuring electrical wiring before walls close

Prevents flaws from occurring during construction.

The shift from QC to QA requires a "total quality cultural shift" - difficult and costly short-term, but beneficial long-term
