Core Principle

The term "cradle" refers to the production phase of a product. "Cradle-to-cradle" transforms the linear "cradle-to-grave" model into a circular system.

Products are designed so that after use, materials are entirely recycled to create new products - eliminating waste through continuous material cycling.

"Like a forest ecosystem: fallen leaves decompose to nourish new growth in a regenerative cycle."

Current Status & Vision

Currently implemented in limited products like certain clothing and office furniture. The concept is still in its infancy.

The Cradle to Cradle Products Innovation Institute launched in 2012, with criteria still evolving.

Growing socio-cultural momentum for sustainability is increasing pressure on companies to design recycled and recyclable products.

Certification Criteria

To receive official C2C certification, products must meet:

  • Material Reutilization: Strict recycling processes
  • Energy for Recycling: Assessment with renewable energy preference
  • Water Use: Evaluation of water consumption
  • Corporate Social Responsibility: Fair labor practices and ethics

Illustrative Example

Designed for the 21st century with recycling and eco-friendliness as core principles:
